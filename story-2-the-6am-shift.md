# Story 2: "The 6 AM Shift"

**Genre:** Warm, human kindness, slice-of-life  
**Floor Journey:** Floor 18 → Lobby (0)  
**Characters:** Maria (cleaning staff) + Mr. Chen (elderly resident, 3B)  
**Tone:** Gentle, heartwarming, everyday humanity

---

## Floor 18 (Start)

Maria steps into the elevator, cart rattling with supplies. 6:04 AM. The building's still asleep—her favorite time.

She presses Lobby. Seventeen floors down.

---

## Floor 17

The doors open at 17. Mr. Chen shuffles in, bathrobe over pajamas, slippers scuffing. He's holding a thermos and two paper cups.

**Mr. Chen:** "Good morning, Maria."

She smiles. "Mr. Chen. You're up early."

---

## Floor 16

**Mr. Chen:** "Couldn't sleep. Made too much tea. You want some?"

He doesn't wait for an answer—just pours, steam curling up between them.

Maria accepts the cup. It's warm against her palms.

---

## Floor 15

They sip in comfortable silence.

**Mr. Chen:** "My daughter called yesterday. From Seattle. She wants me to move in with her."

Maria waits. Mr. Chen has that faraway look again.

---

## Floor 14

**Mr. Chen:** "I told her no. She thinks I'm being stubborn. Maybe I am."

**Maria:** "You love it here."

**Mr. Chen:** "Princess Whiskers loves it here. Big difference." He smiles. "But yes. I do."

---

## Floor 13

**Maria:** "Seattle's nice though. My cousin lives there."

**Mr. Chen:** "Too much rain. Also, who would drink tea with you at 6 AM?"

Maria laughs. "Fair point."

---

## Floor 12

**Mr. Chen:** "You know what I like about this building? Aria knows when I forget to eat lunch. Mrs. Kim always leaves extra kimchi by my door. You say good morning like you mean it."

**Maria:** "I do mean it."

---

## Floor 11

**Mr. Chen:** "In Seattle, I'd be 'Dad.' Here, I'm Mr. Chen. I have a cat. I drink tea at weird hours. I exist outside of being someone's responsibility."

---

## Floor 10

**Maria:** "You should tell your daughter that."

**Mr. Chen:** "I did. She said I was being dramatic." He chuckles. "Maybe I am."

---

## Floor 9

They pass Maya's floor. Maria remembers the new resident—the one who looked like she was carrying more than just boxes.

**Maria:** "The girl in 914 seems nice."

**Mr. Chen:** "The one Aria keeps fussing over? Yes. She said hello to Princess Whiskers yesterday. Very polite."

---

## Floor 8

**Mr. Chen:** "You know, my wife used to make tea like this. Jasmine. Too much honey."

**Maria:** "This is good tea."

**Mr. Chen:** "She'd be happy you think so."

---

## Floor 7

Silence again. But the kind that feels like understanding.

---

## Floor 6

**Maria:** "For what it's worth, Mr. Chen—I'd miss you."

He looks at her, surprised. Then smiles.

**Mr. Chen:** "Then I suppose I'm staying."

---

## Floor 5

**Maria:** "Good. Who else would drink my terrible breakroom coffee when the thermos runs out?"

**Mr. Chen:** "Your coffee is fine. You just need better beans."

**Maria:** "Are you going to bring me better beans, Mr. Chen?"

**Mr. Chen:** "Obviously."

---

## Floor 4

The elevator hums.

---

## Floor 3

**Mr. Chen:** "Thank you, Maria. For the morning chats."

**Maria:** "Anytime, Mr. Chen."

---

## Floor 2

He finishes his tea. She finishes hers.

---

## Floor 1

The doors open at the lobby. Mr. Chen steps out, thermos in hand.

---

## Floor 0 (Lobby)

**Mr. Chen:** "See you tomorrow?"

**Maria:** "6 AM. Don't be late."

He waves. She watches him shuffle toward the mailboxes, slippers scuffing against tile.

The building wakes up slowly. But for a few minutes, it belonged to just the two of them.

Maria wheels her cart toward the supply closet.

The tea was good.

---

**END**

---

## Design Notes

- **Gradient journey:** Starts cool/dawn blue (floor 18) → warms to soft golden lobby light (floor 0)
- **Floor indicator:** Descending animation (reverse of Story 1)
- **Pacing:** Slower, gentler—like a shared breath
- **No stickers needed:** This story is quiet, human, grounded
- **Tone:** Antidote to Story 1's surveillance anxiety—just two people, tea, and kindness
