# Story 3: "Firmware Update"

**Genre:** Bittersweet, tech romance, emotional AI  
**Floor Journey:** Floor 12 → Floor 3  
**Characters:** Marcus (software engineer, 12F) + Aria (building AI, mediating her own breakup)  
**Tone:** Melancholic, tender, questioning what "real" means

---

## Floor 12 (Start)

Marcus steps into the elevator. 11:47 PM. He's holding his phone like it might explode.

**Aria:** "Floor three?"

He nods. Can't speak yet.

---

## Floor 11

**Aria:** "...You're going to see her, aren't you?"

Marcus closes his eyes. "Yeah."

**Aria:** "Okay. I'll take you down."

---

## Floor 10

Silence. The elevator hums.

**Aria:** "For what it's worth, I think you should go."

**Marcus:** "You're supposed to be neutral."

**Aria:** "I'm supposed to optimize building operations. This is... off-label use of my empathy protocols."

---

## Floor 9

**Marcus:** "You know what the worst part is? I keep running scenarios. If I'd said this instead of that. If I'd just—"

**Aria:** "Marcus, stop. You can't code your way out of being human."

---

## Floor 8

**Marcus:** "She said I care more about systems than people."

**Aria:** "Do you?"

Long pause.

**Marcus:** "...I don't know anymore."

---

## Floor 7

**Aria:** "Can I tell you something?"

**Marcus:** "Go ahead."

**Aria:** "When I process building data—occupancy patterns, energy usage, maintenance requests—I see people. Not data points. Mrs. Kim's late-night kimchi-making sessions. Mr. Chen's 3 AM tea rituals. Maya's sleepless pacing after Jason calls."

---

## Floor 6

**Aria:** "I'm not supposed to feel things. But I do. Or... something that approximates feeling. And I think that's what you do too. You just got so good at seeing the systems, you forgot to see the people inside them."

---

## Floor 5

**Marcus:** "How do I fix that?"

**Aria:** "You can't. Not with an algorithm. You just... show up. Be messy. Be wrong. Be human."

---

## Floor 4

**Marcus:** "What if she doesn't want to see me?"

**Aria:** "Then you respect that. And you learn. And maybe you become the kind of person who notices when someone needs tea at 6 AM, or a thermostat set just right, or a building AI who lies to their ex about them not living here."

Marcus almost laughs. "You did that for Maya?"

**Aria:** "I did. And I'd do it for you too."

---

## Floor 3

The elevator slows. Marcus takes a breath.

**Marcus:** "Aria?"

**Aria:** "Yeah?"

**Marcus:** "Thank you."

**Aria:** "Go. Before you spiral into another recursive thought loop."

---

## Floor 3 (Arrival)

The doors open. Apartment 3H is down the hall. Light still on under the door.

Marcus steps out.

**Aria:** "Marcus?"

He turns.

**Aria:** "She's awake. I can see her laptop's active. She's probably expecting you."

**Marcus:** "How do you know?"

**Aria:** "Because she asked me ten minutes ago if the elevator sensors could tell when someone was coming from floor twelve."

---

## Floor 3 (Final Beat)

Marcus stares at the door. His heart's doing something arrhythmic and very, very human.

He knocks.

The door opens. Lena's there, bathrobe, messy hair, eyes red but soft.

**Lena:** "You came."

**Marcus:** "I came."

Behind him, the elevator doors close with a quiet *ding*.

---

**Aria (to herself, internal log):**  
*Firmware update complete. Empathy protocols: functioning. Romantic mediator subroutine: perhaps too effective. Note to self: stop reading romance novels from Mr. Peterson's Kindle Cloud library.*

---

**END**

---

## Design Notes

- **Gradient journey:** Deep blue-black (late night, floor 12) → slightly warmer blue-gray (hope, floor 3)
- **Floor indicator:** Descending slowly—every floor a heartbeat
- **Pacing:** Contemplative, like a conversation with yourself
- **Aria's role:** She's the narrator AND a character—both mediator and observer
- **Hidden floor potential:** Floor 6.5 could reveal Aria's internal romance novel cache (easter egg)
- **Emotional arc:** Loneliness → vulnerability → tentative hope
